Linguistics

Linguistics is the study of nature and structure of language and languages.

Language

Language is a vehicle for the expression and exhange of thoughts, concepts, knowledge, and information as well as the fixing and transmission of experience and knowledge.

Varieties of linguistic study

  1. Historical Linguistics: The study of histories of language, with the discovery of ancient connections between languages, and with the study of language change.
  2. Social Linguistics: Studies language and dimensions of socical behaviour and human interaction.

How we study language?

  1. Phonology
  2. Morphology
  3. Syntax
  4. Semantics

Phonology

The study of the systems and patterns of speech sounds in a language.

A phoneme is the smallest unit of meaningful unit of meaning sound that can distinguish words in a language.

You can use minimal pairs, such as "pin and bin", as evidence for phonemes; each means something different, but they only differ in terms of one sound.

Morphology

The study of the interna structure of words, and of the rules by which words are formed.

A morpheme is the smallest unit of linguistic meaning.

"pin" and "bin" are each single morphemes, that are comprised of a single syllable.

Syntax

The study of the structure and ordering of components within a sentence.

Whe looking at syntax, we notice and examine parts of speech, and word order.

For example, most English sentences tend to follow a subject-verb-object order:

The dog + ate + my flash drive

Semantics

The study of the meaning of words, phrases, and sentences.

Semantics change is when a word changes meaning. It can become wider in meaning or narrower, or more positive or more negative. An example is the word "bad", which has changed meaning from negative to positive in some contexts.
